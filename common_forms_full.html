Common forms of manipulation of the truth and the potential repercussions

Discussing COVID-19 in an accurate and age appropriate manner can protect your dependants short term physical health and their long term psychological health.

When discussing COVID-19 with your dependant, it is important that you are having an honest and age appropriate conversation. You may feel the need to tell white lies or manipulate the truth to protect your dependant from the effects of the pandemic. However, these lies can increase your dependants risk of contracting COVID-19 as well as interfere with their long term psychological health (Dalton, Rapa & Stein, 2020).

As a responsible adult, it is your responsibility to ensure your dependant understands the world around them. The pandemic has caused the world to shift and change in an unprecedented way. For this reason, your dependant may be feeling anxious, overwhelmed or even scared for the future. Prolonged periods of stress can lead to Acute stress disorder (ASD) and if left untreated can lead to Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTDS) (Roccella, 2020).
