What is misinformation?

According to the Oxford Dictionary, misinformation refers to “false or inaccurate information, especially that which is deliberately intended to deceive”. With a large amount of information available about COVID-19, it is often difficult to easily and reliably ensure if the information an individual is accessing is accurate. Misinformation can also be passed between responsible adults and their dependants as white lies and the manipulation of truth. These lies or half truths can be interpreted by dependants as fact and spread to the wider community. It is important that responsible adults are sharing accurate, honest and age appropriate information with their dependants in regards to COVID-19.

Visualisation of how misinformation is spread. Source: World Health Organisation (WHO)

Personalised information for responsible adults

Every responsible adult has a unique parenting approach and it is imperative this individual is sharing age appropriate information to their dependant. For this reason, the Australian Government has provided this information package that responds to the unique dynamics of each responsible adult and dependent relationship. The Australian Government also recognises that responsible adults know their child best. This information package is to be used as a guide and can be adjusted based on your dependants needs.

Often responsible adults feel that they need to manipulate the truth in order to protect their dependant from the realities of COVID-19. However, this can increase the dependants short term risk of contracting COVID-19 and affect their long term psychological health (Dalton, Rapa & Stein, 2020). The most effective way to protect dependants is by communicating in an accurate, honest and age appropriate manner when discussing COVID-19.
